Teaching assistant, Fall 2024. Supported 40+ undergraduates through process management, memory allocation,
and concurrency.

- Designed and graded programming assignments on system calls and synchronization
- Facilitated weekly problem-solving sessions
- Assisted in developing midterm examination questions
- Held office hours

Concurrency was the concept students found hardest, because a race condition is invisible — the code looks
correct and usually runs correctly, until one time it does not. Explaining critical sections abstractly did
not work. What did was having students run a deliberately broken counter a thousand times and watch the
output drift. Once they had seen the bug with their own eyes, mutexes and semaphores stopped being
vocabulary to memorise and became a fix for something real.
