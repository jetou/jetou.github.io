Inverse problems in the physical sciences are frequently ill-posed: many candidate solutions fit the
observations equally well, and the prior knowledge that would ordinarily break the tie is itself
incomplete. This work explores how diffusion-based methods can support active inference in exactly that
setting — choosing what to measure next when the prior cannot be trusted to disambiguate on its own.
