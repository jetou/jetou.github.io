No single variable ordering heuristic dominates across constraint satisfaction problems, and the right
choice often depends on structure that only becomes visible partway through the search. This work
represents the shape of the early search tree as a sequence, predicts the eventual number of failures from
it, and uses that prediction to pick a heuristic — identifying an effective one for 86% of hard instances
and outperforming state-of-the-art adaptive methods on both solution rate and runtime.
