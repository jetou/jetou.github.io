My doctoral research applies machine learning to inference problems in hadronic physics — recovering physical distributions from raw scattering events, representing parton distributions with neural networks rather than hand-chosen functional forms, and doing active inference when the available priors are incomplete.

Before the Ph.D., my research was in combinatorial optimization: learning which search heuristic to apply, and finding promising regions of search spaces too large to explore exhaustively.
